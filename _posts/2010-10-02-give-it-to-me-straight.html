Let's start with first principles. I want to know things; I want to know almost everything I can. In all but a select few scenarios I'll put in the time to learn something vs not.

I'm not sure how this all got started, but it feels like nobody wants to explain anything anymore. I see it among friends, strangers, between parents and their children, all over.

Let's take a ride in the wayback machine.... *whirrrrrrr*

Way back in the day, when I was a wee little high school student, I played the guitar. We all did. If you want to get laid in high school, you join the football team or you play guitar. I played guitar.

There's actually one more step: you have to play for somebody.

So there I was.

Some kind soul had agreed to let us, a rowdy group of suburban teens, make loud, awesome noise for a few hours. And the guitar slingers among us were getting ready to melt faces.

I doubt this particular individual understood the complex set of motives for our playing guitars (scene cred, getting laid, or both), but that's a story for another time.

One particular guy, let's call him Jake, was sitting alone with his guitar doing something I'd never seen before, tuning with harmonics. It's really nothing special, but I was young and stupid and my motives were more than a little misplaced.

Even despite that, I like to know things. I asked about it.

"It's just a dumb trick," Jake tells me.

We went back and forth for a while, him telling me it's nothing and trying to get me to leave, and my pressing for more information.

I eventually got him to tell me it was the 5th and 7th fret harmonics, and you can use them to tune a guitar (in so many words). At the risk of being a huge hypocrite, I'm going to skip over that for now as it's not relevant to my story, but you can learn how on youtube.

That isn't a particularly uncommon occurence. Some people just don't want to explain anything.

I have a couple ideas why.

Maybe Jake is impatient and he doesn't want to waste his time teaching whenever he knows anything.

Maybe he's shy, and embarrassed by suddenly becoming the authority on this one infinitesimally small topic.

Whatever it is, he'd have saved us both some time by just spitting out the one sentence explanation. If it's the former he's doing a disservice to the world, and in the case of the latter, to himself.

Google is great, but half the battle is knowing what to search for in the first place. It's usually the hardest step and presents a pretty big barrier to entry when you're first learning about anything.

Until google can read minds, we still need other people to help us along the way. So don't dumb it down for me. Give it to me straight.

(In other news, it turns out communication skills are useful for all kinds of things)
